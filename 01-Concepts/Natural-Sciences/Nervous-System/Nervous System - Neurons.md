# Neurons and Nerve Impulses

## Neuron Structure

### Cell Body (Soma)
- Contains nucleus
- Metabolic center

### Dendrites
- Receive signals from other neurons
- Multiple branches
- Input zone

### Axon
- Transmits signals away from cell body
- Can be very long (up to 1 meter)
- Covered by myelin sheath (insulation)
- Output zone

### Axon Terminal
- Contains synaptic vesicles
- Releases neurotransmitters
- Forms synapses with other cells

## Types of Neurons

### Sensory (Afferent)
- Carry signals TO central nervous system
- From receptors to CNS

### Motor (Efferent)
- Carry signals FROM central nervous system
- From CNS to effectors (muscles, glands)

### Interneurons
- Connect neurons within CNS
- Integration and processing

## Resting Potential
- Neuron at rest: -70 mV (inside negative)
- Maintained by $Na^+$/$K^+$ pump
- 3 $Na^+$ out, 2 $K^+$ in (uses ATP)

## Action Potential

### 1. Depolarization
- Stimulus opens $Na^+$ channels
- $Na^+$ rushes in
- Membrane potential becomes positive (+30 mV)

### 2. Repolarization
- $K^+$ channels open
- $K^+$ flows out
- Returns toward negative

### 3. Hyperpolarization
- Membrane briefly more negative than resting
- Refractory period (cannot fire again)

### 4. Return to Resting
- $Na^+$/$K^+$ pump restores balance

## All-or-None Principle
Action potential either occurs fully or not at all
Strength of stimulus affects frequency, not amplitude

## Propagation Speed
Factors increasing speed:
- **Myelination**: Saltatory conduction (jumps between nodes)
- **Axon diameter**: Larger = faster

## Synapse

### Electrical Synapse
- Direct connection via gap junctions
- Fast, bidirectional
- Rare in humans

### Chemical Synapse
1. Action potential reaches axon terminal
2. Ca**2⁺ enters terminal
3. Vesicles fuse with membrane
4. Neurotransmitter released into synaptic cleft
5. Binds to receptors on postsynaptic neuron
6. Opens ion channels
7. Generates postsynaptic potential

## Neurotransmitters

### Excitatory
- **Acetylcholine**: Muscle contraction, memory
- **Glutamate**: Main excitatory in CNS
- **Dopamine**: Reward, movement

### Inhibitory
- **GABA**: Main inhibitory in CNS
- **Glycine**: Inhibitory in spinal cord

### Modulatory
- **Serotonin**: Mood, sleep
- **Norepinephrine**: Alertness, arousal
