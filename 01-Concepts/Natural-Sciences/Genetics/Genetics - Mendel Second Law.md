# Mendel's Second Law (Law of Independent Assortment)

## Core Principle
Alleles for different genes segregate **independently** during gamete formation. The inheritance of one trait does not affect the inheritance of another.

## Key Concepts

### Dihybrid Cross
Cross involving two traits simultaneously (e.g., seed shape AND seed color)

### Example: RrYy * RrYy
- R = Round (dominant), r = wrinkled
- Y = Yellow (dominant), y = green

**Phenotype ratio**: 9:3:3:1
- 9 Round Yellow
- 3 Round green
- 3 wrinkled Yellow
- 1 wrinkled green

## Gamete Formation
RrYy parent produces 4 gamete types: RY, Ry, rY, ry (each 25%)

## 16-Square Punnett Square

```
        RY      Ry      rY      ry
RY    RRYY    RRYy    RrYY    RrYy
Ry    RRYy    RRyy    RrYy    Rryy
rY    RrYY    RrYy    rrYY    rrYy
ry    RrYy    Rryy    rrYy    rryy
```

## When It Doesn't Apply
- Linked genes (on same chromosome)
- Genes close together don't assort independently
