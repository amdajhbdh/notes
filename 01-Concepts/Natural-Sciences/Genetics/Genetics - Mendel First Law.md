# Mendel's First Law (Law of Segregation)

## Core Principle
During gamete formation, the two alleles for each gene **separate** so that each gamete receives only one allele. Offspring inherit one allele from each parent.

## Key Concepts

### Alleles
- **Allele**: Alternative forms of a gene
- **Dominant allele**: Expressed when present (uppercase, e.g., A)
- **Recessive allele**: Only expressed when homozygous (lowercase, e.g., a)

### Genotype vs Phenotype
- **Genotype**: Genetic makeup (AA, Aa, aa)
- **Phenotype**: Observable trait
- **Homozygous**: Two identical alleles (AA or aa)
- **Heterozygous**: Two different alleles (Aa)

## Punnett Square Example

**Cross**: Aa * Aa

```
     A    a
A   AA   Aa
a   Aa   aa
```

**Results**:
- Genotype ratio: 1 AA : 2 Aa : 1 aa
- Phenotype ratio: 3 dominant : 1 recessive

## Mendel's Pea Plant Experiments
- Trait studied: Seed shape (Round vs Wrinkled)
- P generation: RR * rr
- F1 generation: All Rr (round)
- F2 generation: 3 round : 1 wrinkled

## Test Cross
Cross an unknown genotype with homozygous recessive (aa) to determine if it's AA or Aa.

## Practice Questions
1. If both parents are Aa, what's the probability of aa offspring?
2. A tall plant (Tt) crosses with a short plant (tt). What are the offspring ratios?
