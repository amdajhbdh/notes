# DNA Replication

## Overview
Process by which DNA makes an exact copy of itself before cell division. Ensures genetic information passes to daughter cells.

## Key Principle: Semi-Conservative Replication
Each new DNA molecule contains:
- One **original** (template) strand
- One **newly synthesized** strand

## Replication Process

### 1. Initiation
**Origin of Replication**
- Helicase unwinds the double helix
- Creates replication fork (Y-shaped structure)
- Single-strand binding proteins (SSB) stabilize separated strands

### 2. Elongation

**Leading Strand** (5'  ->  3')
- Synthesized continuously
- DNA polymerase III adds nucleotides
- Follows replication fork smoothly

**Lagging Strand** (3'  ->  5')
- Synthesized discontinuously
- Creates Okazaki fragments (short segments)
- Synthesized away from replication fork

**Key Enzymes:**
- **Primase**: Adds RNA primer (starting point)
- **DNA Polymerase III**: Main synthesis enzyme
- **DNA Polymerase I**: Removes primers, fills gaps
- **Ligase**: Joins Okazaki fragments

### 3. Termination
- Replication forks meet
- All primers removed and gaps filled
- Two identical DNA molecules formed

## Direction of Synthesis
DNA polymerase can only add nucleotides in **5'  ->  3' direction**

## Proofreading
- DNA polymerase has 3'  ->  5' exonuclease activity
- Checks and corrects errors
- Error rate: ~1 in 10 billion bases

## Energy Source
- dNTPs (deoxynucleoside triphosphates)
- Breaking phosphate bonds provides energy

## Speed
- Prokaryotes: ~1000 nucleotides/second
- Eukaryotes: ~50 nucleotides/second
