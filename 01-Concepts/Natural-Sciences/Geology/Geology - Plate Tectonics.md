# Plate Tectonics

## Theory Overview
Earth's lithosphere is divided into large plates that move on the asthenosphere.

## Earth's Layers

### Crust
- Oceanic: 5-10 km thick, basaltic, denser
- Continental: 30-70 km thick, granitic, less dense

### Mantle
- Upper mantle: Rigid (part of lithosphere)
- Asthenosphere: Plastic, flowing layer
- Lower mantle: Solid but can flow slowly

### Core
- Outer core: Liquid iron and nickel
- Inner core: Solid iron and nickel

## Tectonic Plates
Major plates:
- Pacific, North American, Eurasian, African
- South American, Antarctic, Indo-Australian

## Plate Boundaries

### 1. Divergent Boundaries
Plates move apart

**Features**:
- Mid-ocean ridges
- Rift valleys
- New crust forms (seafloor spreading)
- Shallow earthquakes
- Volcanic activity

**Example**: Mid-Atlantic Ridge

### 2. Convergent Boundaries
Plates move together

**Types**:

**Oceanic-Continental**:
- Oceanic subducts (denser)
- Deep ocean trenches
- Volcanic mountain ranges
- Example: Andes Mountains

**Oceanic-Oceanic**:
- One subducts
- Island arcs form
- Example: Japan, Philippines

**Continental-Continental**:
- Neither subducts
- Mountain ranges form
- Example: Himalayas

### 3. Transform Boundaries
Plates slide past each other

**Features**:
- Earthquakes (no volcanoes)
- Fault lines
- Example: San Andreas Fault

## Driving Forces

### Convection Currents
- Heat from core causes mantle circulation
- Hot material rises, cool sinks
- Drags plates along

### Ridge Push
- Elevated mid-ocean ridges
- Gravity pulls plates downward

### Slab Pull
- Dense oceanic plate sinks
- Pulls rest of plate with it
- Most powerful force

## Evidence for Plate Tectonics

### Continental Fit
- Coastlines match (Africa & South America)

### Fossil Distribution
- Same fossils on different continents
- Example: Mesosaurus in Africa and South America

### Rock Types
- Similar rock formations across continents

### Paleomagnetic Data
- Magnetic stripes on ocean floor
- Symmetrical pattern from mid-ocean ridges

### Earthquake & Volcano Distribution
- Concentrated at plate boundaries

## Consequences

### Earthquakes
- Most at plate boundaries
- Transform and convergent most active

### Volcanoes
- Ring of Fire (Pacific plate boundaries)
- Subduction zones
- Mid-ocean ridges

### Mountain Building
- Convergent boundaries
- Continental collision

### Ocean Basins
- Divergent boundaries
- Seafloor spreading
