# Newton's Laws of Motion

## First Law (Law of Inertia)
An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.

**Formula**: If ΣF = 0, then v = constant

**Inertia**: Resistance to change in motion (depends on mass)

## Second Law (F = ma)
The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass.

**Formula**: ΣF = ma

Where:
- ΣF = net force (N)
- m = mass (kg)
- a = acceleration (m/s**2)

**Vector Form**: F⃗ = ma⃗

## Third Law (Action-Reaction)
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

**Formula**: F_1_2 = -F_2_1

Key points:
- Forces act on different objects
- Equal magnitude, opposite direction
- Simultaneous occurrence

## Applications

### Free Fall
F = mg (weight)
a = g = 9.8 m/s**2

### Inclined Plane
- Parallel component: F∥ = mg sin(θ)
- Perpendicular: F⊥ = mg cos(θ)

### Connected Objects
Analyze each object separately, then combine equations

## Problem-Solving Strategy
1. Draw free body diagram
2. Choose coordinate system
3. Apply ΣF = ma for each direction
4. Solve system of equations

## Examples

**Example 1**: 10 kg object with 50 N force
a = F/m = 50/10 = 5 m/s**2

**Example 2**: Object on table
Normal force N = mg (balances weight)
