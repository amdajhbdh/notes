# Complex Numbers - Conjugates

## Definition

The **complex conjugate** of a complex number z = a + bi is defined as z̄ = a - bi, where a and b are real numbers.

## Properties

1. $(\overline{z_1 + z_2}) = \overline{z_1} + \overline{z_2}$
2. $(\overline{z_1 \cdot z_2}) = \overline{z_1} \cdot \overline{z_2}$
3. $\overline{\overline{z}} = z$
4. $z \cdot \overline{z} = |z|^2$

## Geometric Interpretation

The complex conjugate represents a reflection of the complex number across the real axis in the complex plane.

## Applications

- Simplifying complex fractions
- Finding modulus of complex numbers
- Solving polynomial equations with real coefficients

## Examples

### Example 1
Find the conjugate of $z = 3 + 4i$
Solution: $\overline{z} = 3 - 4i$

### Example 2
Simplify $\frac{2 + 3i}{1 + i}$
Solution: Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of denominator
$$\frac{2 + 3i}{1 + i} \cdot \frac{1 - i}{1 - i} = \frac{(2 + 3i)(1 - i)}{(1 + i)(1 - i)} = \frac{2 - 2i + 3i - 3i^2}{1 - i^2} = \frac{5 + i}{2}$$
