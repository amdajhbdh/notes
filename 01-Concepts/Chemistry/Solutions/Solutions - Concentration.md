# Solution Concentration

## Definitions

**Solution** = Solute + Solvent
- Solute: Substance being dissolved
- Solvent: Substance doing the dissolving

## Concentration Units

### 1. Molarity (M)
Moles of solute per liter of solution
**Formula**: ppm = (mass_solute/mass_solution) * 10**6

Used for very dilute solutions

### 6. Mole Fraction (χ)
**Formula**: χ_A = n_A/(n_A + n_B + ...)

Sum of all mole fractions = 1

## Dilution Formula
M_1V_1 = M_2V_2

Where:
- M_1 = initial molarity
- V_1 = initial volume
- M_2 = final molarity
- V_2 = final volume

## Conversions

### Mass to Moles
n = m/M (M = molar mass)

### Molarity to Molality
Need density of solution

## Examples

**Example 1**: Make 0.5 M NaCl, 2 L
n = M×V = 0.5×2 = 1 mol
mass = 1×58.5 = 58.5 g NaCl needed

**Example 2**: Dilute 100 mL of 6 M HCl to 2 M
M_1V_1 = M_2V_2
6×100 = 2×V_2
V_2 = 300 mL (add 200 mL water)
