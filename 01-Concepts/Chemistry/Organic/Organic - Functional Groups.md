# Functional Groups

## Definition
Specific groups of atoms that determine chemical properties and reactivity of organic molecules.

## Major Functional Groups

### 1. Hydroxyl Group (-OH)
**Alcohols**: R-OH

**Examples**:
- Methanol: $CH_3OH$
- Ethanol: $C_2H_5OH$
- Propanol: $C_3H_7OH$

**Properties**:
- Polar, forms hydrogen bonds
- Soluble in water (small alcohols)
- Higher boiling points than alkanes

**Nomenclature**: -ol suffix
- Methanol, ethanol, propanol

### 2. Carbonyl Group (C=O)

#### Aldehydes: R-CHO
Terminal carbonyl (at end of chain)

**Examples**:
- Formaldehyde: $HCHO$
- Acetaldehyde: $CH_3CHO$

**Nomenclature**: -al suffix
- Methanal, ethanal, propanal

#### Ketones: R-CO-R'
Internal carbonyl (middle of chain)

**Examples**:
- Acetone: $CH_3COCH_3$
- Butanone: $CH_3COC_2H_5$

**Nomenclature**: -one suffix
- Propanone, butanone

### 3. Carboxyl Group (-COOH)
**Carboxylic Acids**: R-COOH

**Examples**:
- Formic acid: $HCOOH$
- Acetic acid: $CH_3COOH$
- Benzoic acid: $C_6H_5COOH$

**Properties**:
- Acidic (donate H+)
- Form salts with bases
- Hydrogen bonding

**Nomenclature**: -oic acid suffix
- Methanoic acid, ethanoic acid

### 4. Ester Group (-COO-)
**Esters**: R-COO-R'

Formed from acid + alcohol

**Examples**:
- Methyl acetate: $CH_3COOCH_3$
- Ethyl acetate: $CH_3COOC_2H_5$

**Properties**:
- Pleasant fruity odors
- Used in perfumes, flavors
- Less polar than acids

**Nomenclature**: alkyl alkanoate
- Methyl methanoate, ethyl ethanoate

### 5. Amino Group (-NH_2)
**Amines**: R-NH_2 (primary)

**Examples**:
- Methylamine: $CH_3NH_2$
- Aniline: $C_6H_5NH_2$

**Properties**:
- Basic (accept H+)
- Fishy odor
- Hydrogen bonding

**Classification**:
- Primary: R-NH_2
- Secondary: R-NH-R'
- Tertiary: R-N(R')(R'')

### 6. Amide Group (-CONH_2)
**Amides**: R-CONH_2

Formed from acid + amine

**Examples**:
- Acetamide: $CH_3CONH_2$
- Urea: $(NH_2)_2CO$

**Properties**:
- Found in proteins (peptide bonds)
- High boiling points
- Neutral (not basic like amines)

### 7. Ether Group (-O-)
**Ethers**: R-O-R'

**Examples**:
- Dimethyl ether: $CH_3OCH_3$
- Diethyl ether: $C_2H_5OC_2H_5$

**Properties**:
- Less reactive than alcohols
- Good solvents
- Lower boiling points than alcohols

### 8. Halides (-X)
**Alkyl Halides**: R-X (X = F, Cl, Br, I)

**Examples**:
- Chloromethane: $CH_3Cl$
- Bromoethane: $C_2H_5Br$

**Properties**:
- Polar C-X bond
- Reactive (substitution reactions)
- Used as solvents, refrigerants

### 9. Nitro Group (-NO_2)
**Nitro Compounds**: R-NO_2

**Examples**:
- Nitromethane: $CH_3NO_2$
- Nitrobenzene: $C_6H_5NO_2$

**Properties**:
- Electron-withdrawing
- Explosive potential
- Yellow color

### 10. Sulfhydryl Group (-SH)
**Thiols**: R-SH

**Examples**:
- Methanethiol: $CH_3SH$
- Ethanethiol: $C_2H_5SH$

**Properties**:
- Strong unpleasant odor
- Similar to alcohols
- Important in proteins (cysteine)

## Priority Order (IUPAC)

When multiple groups present, highest priority determines suffix:

1. Carboxylic acid (-COOH)
2. Ester (-COO-)
3. Amide (-CONH_2)
4. Aldehyde (-CHO)
5. Ketone (-CO-)
6. Alcohol (-OH)
7. Amine (-NH_2)
8. Ether (-O-)
9. Halide (-X)

Lower priority groups become prefixes.

## Functional Group Reactions

### Oxidation Levels
Alkane  ->  Alcohol  ->  Aldehyde  ->  Carboxylic Acid

**Example**:
$$CH_3CH_3 \rightarrow CH_3CH_2OH \rightarrow CH_3CHO \rightarrow CH_3COOH$$

### Esterification
Acid + Alcohol  ->  Ester + Water

$$CH_3COOH + C_2H_5OH \rightarrow CH_3COOC_2H_5 + H_2O$$

### Amide Formation
Acid + Amine  ->  Amide + Water

$$CH_3COOH + NH_3 \rightarrow CH_3CONH_2 + H_2O$$

## Polyfunctional Molecules

### Example: Amino Acids
Contains both -COOH and -NH_2

Glycine: $H_2NCH_2COOH$

### Example: Hydroxy Acids
Contains both -COOH and -OH

Lactic acid: $CH_3CH(OH)COOH$

## Practice Problems

### Problem 1
Identify functional groups in: $CH_3CH_2COOH$

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>
Carboxyl group (-COOH) - Carboxylic acid
Name: Propanoic acid
</details>

### Problem 2
Name: $CH_3COCH_3$

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>
Ketone (carbonyl in middle)
Name: Propanone (acetone)
</details>

### Problem 3
What functional group forms when ethanol is oxidized?

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>
First oxidation: Aldehyde (ethanal)
Further oxidation: Carboxylic acid (ethanoic acid)
</details>
