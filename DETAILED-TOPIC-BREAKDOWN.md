## 🧪 CHEMISTRY TOPICS

### TOPIC 1: ACID-BASE CHEMISTRY (CRITICAL - 812 occurrences)

#### What You Need to Know

**1. pH Definition & Calculation**
```
pH = -log[H⁺]
pOH = -log[OH⁻]
pH + pOH = 14 (at 25°C)
```

**2. Strong Acids & Bases**
- Strong acids: HCl, HBr, HI, HNO₃, H₂SO₄, HClO₄
- Strong bases: NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)₂, Ba(OH)₂
- For strong acids: [H⁺] = concentration
- For strong bases: [OH⁻] = concentration

**3. Weak Acids & Bases**
- Ka = [H⁺][A⁻]/[HA]
- pKa = -log(Ka)
- For weak acids: pH ≈ ½(pKa - log C)
- Henderson-Hasselbalch: pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA])

**4. Buffer Solutions**
- Buffer = weak acid + conjugate base (or weak base + conjugate acid)
- Buffer capacity depends on concentration
- Optimal pH = pKa (when [HA] = [A⁻])
- Buffer resists pH changes

**5. Titration**
- Equivalence point: moles acid = moles base
- Indicator changes color at equivalence point
- Titration curve has 4 regions:
  1. Initial pH (before equivalence)
  2. Buffer region (before equivalence)
  3. Equivalence point (steep rise)
  4. Excess base (after equivalence)

**6. Common Indicators**
| Indicator | Color Change | pH Range |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Methyl orange | Red → Yellow | 3.1-4.4 |
| Methyl red | Red → Yellow | 4.4-6.2 |
| Bromothymol blue | Yellow → Blue | 6.0-7.6 |
| Phenolphthalein | Colorless → Pink | 8.2-10.0 |

#### Exam Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: pH Calculation**
- "Calculate the pH of 0.1 M HCl solution"
- "Find the pH of a buffer containing 0.1 M acetic acid and 0.05 M acetate"

**Pattern 2: Titration Problems**
- "25 mL of HCl is titrated with 0.1 M NaOH. 20 mL of NaOH is needed. Find [HCl]"
- "Determine the concentration of the acid using the titration data"

**Pattern 3: Buffer Problems**
- "Calculate the pH change when 0.01 mol HCl is added to 1 L buffer"
- "What mass of NaOH must be added to prepare a buffer of pH 4.5?"

**Pattern 4: Indicator Selection**
- "Which indicator is suitable for titrating a weak acid with strong base?"
- "Explain why phenolphthalein is used for this titration"

#### Practice Strategy
1. Memorize all strong acids and bases
2. Master pH calculations (do 50 problems)
3. Understand buffer equations
4. Practice titration calculations (do 30 problems)
5. Learn indicator color changes

### TOPIC 2: ORGANIC CHEMISTRY (211 occurrences)

#### Functional Groups to Master

**1. Alcohols (R-OH)**
- Primary: R-CH₂-OH (oxidizes to aldehyde)
- Secondary: R-CHOH-R' (oxidizes to ketone)
- Tertiary: R-C(OH)-R'R'' (doesn't oxidize)

**Reactions:**
- Oxidation: Alcohol → Aldehyde/Ketone → Carboxylic acid
- Dehydration: Alcohol → Alkene (with H₂SO₄)
- Esterification: Alcohol + Carboxylic acid → Ester

**2. Aldehydes (R-CHO)**
- Oxidation: Aldehyde → Carboxylic acid
- Reduction: Aldehyde → Alcohol
- Condensation: Aldehyde + Amine → Imine

**3. Ketones (R-CO-R')**
- Similar to aldehydes but less reactive
- Oxidation: Ketone → Carboxylic acid (with strong oxidizer)
- Reduction: Ketone → Alcohol

**4. Carboxylic Acids (R-COOH)**
- Acidic (pKa ≈ 4-5)
- Esterification: Acid + Alcohol → Ester + Water
- Decarboxylation: Acid → Alkane + CO₂

**5. Esters (R-COO-R')**
- Hydrolysis: Ester + Water → Acid + Alcohol
- Saponification: Ester + NaOH → Soap + Alcohol

**6. Amines (R-NH₂)**
- Basic (pKb ≈ 9-10)
- Amide formation: Amine + Acid → Amide

#### Compound Identification Tests

| Test | Positive Result | Identifies |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| 2,4-DNP | Yellow precipitate | Aldehyde or ketone |
| Tollens | Silver mirror | Aldehyde only |
| Fehling | Red precipitate | Aldehyde only |
| Bromine water | Decolorizes | Alkene or phenol |
| Permanganate | Decolorizes | Alkene or alcohol |

#### Exam Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: Compound Identification**
- "A compound gives a yellow precipitate with 2,4-DNP and a silver mirror with Tollens. Identify it."
- Answer: Aldehyde (both tests positive)

**Pattern 2: Reaction Sequences**
- "Compound A (C₄H₈O) undergoes oxidation to form B (C₄H₈O₂). Identify A and B."
- Answer: A = alcohol, B = aldehyde or ketone

**Pattern 3: Synthesis Problems**
- "Show how to prepare an ester from an alcohol and carboxylic acid"
- Answer: Esterification reaction with H₂SO₄ catalyst

#### Practice Strategy
1. Learn all functional groups
2. Memorize test reactions
3. Practice compound identification (do 40 problems)
4. Draw reaction mechanisms
5. Solve synthesis problems

### TOPIC 3: KINETICS & REDOX (130 occurrences)

#### Kinetics

**Reaction Rate Definition:**
- Average rate = Δ[concentration]/Δt
- Instantaneous rate = d[concentration]/dt

**Factors Affecting Rate:**
1. **Concentration:** Higher concentration → faster rate
2. **Temperature:** Higher temperature → faster rate (Arrhenius equation)
3. **Catalyst:** Catalyst → faster rate (lowers activation energy)
4. **Surface area:** Larger surface → faster rate

**Rate Laws:**
- Rate = k[A]^m[B]^n
- k = rate constant
- m, n = reaction orders (determined experimentally)

**Half-Life:**
- For first-order: t₁/₂ = 0.693/k
- For second-order: t₁/₂ = 1/(k[A]₀)

#### Redox Reactions

**Oxidation States:**
- Element in compound: assign oxidation state
- Rules: O = -2, H = +1, alkali metals = +1, halogens = -1

**Balancing Redox Equations:**
1. Identify oxidation state changes
2. Write half-reactions (oxidation and reduction)
3. Balance atoms (except O and H)
4. Balance O with H₂O
5. Balance H with H⁺
6. Balance charge with electrons
7. Multiply to equalize electrons
8. Add half-reactions

#### Exam Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: Rate Calculations**
- "Calculate the reaction rate from concentration vs. time data"
- "Determine the rate constant from the rate law"

**Pattern 2: Catalyst Effects**
- "Explain how a catalyst affects reaction rate"
- "Compare reaction rates with and without catalyst"

**Pattern 3: Redox Balancing**
- "Balance the redox equation: MnO₄⁻ + Fe²⁺ → Mn²⁺ + Fe³⁺"

#### Practice Strategy
1. Master rate calculations (do 25 problems)
2. Learn oxidation state rules
3. Practice redox balancing (do 30 problems)
4. Understand catalyst effects
5. Calculate half-lives

## ⚡ PHYSICS TOPICS

### TOPIC 4: ELECTROMAGNETISM & INDUCTION (262 occurrences)

#### Magnetic Fields

**Magnetic Field Strength:**
- B = μ₀I/(2πr) (around a wire)
- B = μ₀nI (inside a solenoid)
- B = μ₀I/(2R) (at center of circular loop)

**Magnetic Flux:**
- Φ = BA cos(θ)
- Φ = B·A (when perpendicular)

#### Faraday's Law of Induction

**Induced EMF:**
- ε = -dΦ/dt
- ε = -N(dΦ/dt) (for N turns)

**Lenz's Law:**
- Induced current opposes the change in flux
- Direction determined by right-hand rule

#### Self-Inductance

**Inductance Definition:**
- L = Φ/I
- L = μ₀N²A/l (for solenoid)

**Induced EMF in Inductor:**
- ε = -L(dI/dt)

**Energy Stored:**
- E = ½LI²

#### Exam Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: Magnetic Field Calculations**
- "Calculate the magnetic field inside a solenoid with 1000 turns, 0.1 m length, 2 A current"
- Answer: B = μ₀nI = (4π×10⁻⁷)(10000)(2) = 0.0251 T

**Pattern 2: Induction Problems**
- "A coil with 800 turns experiences a flux change from 0.1 to 0 Wb in 0.2 s. Find induced EMF"
- Answer: ε = N(ΔΦ/Δt) = 800(0.1/0.2) = 400 V

**Pattern 3: Inductance Calculations**
- "Find the inductance of a solenoid: 1000 turns, 0.1 m length, 0.01 m² area"
- Answer: L = μ₀N²A/l = (4π×10⁻⁷)(10⁶)(0.01)/0.1 = 0.0126 H

#### Practice Strategy
1. Memorize all formulas
2. Practice field calculations (do 20 problems)
3. Master Faraday's law (do 25 problems)
4. Understand Lenz's law
5. Calculate inductance values

### TOPIC 5: AC CIRCUITS (97 occurrences)

#### RLC Circuits

**Impedance:**
- Z = √(R² + (XL - XC)²)
- XL = ωL = 2πfL (inductive reactance)
- XC = 1/(ωC) = 1/(2πfC) (capacitive reactance)

**Resonance:**
- Occurs when XL = XC
- Resonance frequency: f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)
- At resonance: Z = R (minimum)
- At resonance: I = V/R (maximum)

**Phase Angle:**
- tan(φ) = (XL - XC)/R
- φ = 0 at resonance
- φ > 0 when XL > XC (inductive)
- φ < 0 when XC > XL (capacitive)

**Power:**
- P = VI cos(φ) (real power)
- cos(φ) = power factor

#### Phasor Diagrams

**Construction:**
1. Draw R along positive x-axis
2. Draw XL upward (positive y)
3. Draw XC downward (negative y)
4. Resultant = impedance Z

#### Exam Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: Impedance Calculations**
- "Calculate impedance of RLC circuit: R=42Ω, L=0.4H, C=10μF at f=50Hz"

**Pattern 2: Resonance Problems**
- "Find resonance frequency of RLC circuit"
- "Calculate maximum current at resonance"

**Pattern 3: Phase Relationships**
- "Determine if circuit is inductive or capacitive"
- "Calculate phase angle between voltage and current"

#### Practice Strategy
1. Master impedance calculations (do 20 problems)
2. Find resonance frequencies (do 15 problems)
3. Draw phasor diagrams (do 20 problems)
4. Understand phase relationships
5. Calculate power factors

### TOPIC 6: MECHANICS (89 occurrences)

#### Energy Conservation

**Mechanical Energy:**
- E = KE + PE = ½mv² + mgh
- E = constant (no friction)

**Work-Energy Theorem:**
- W = ΔKE = ½m(v₂² - v₁²)

#### Spring Systems

**Hooke's Law:**
- F = -kx
- k = spring constant

**Spring Potential Energy:**
- PE = ½kx²

**Oscillation Period:**
- T = 2π√(m/k)
- f = 1/T

#### Projectile Motion

**Horizontal Motion:**
- x = v₀ₓt
- vₓ = v₀ₓ (constant)

**Vertical Motion:**
- y = v₀ᵧt - ½gt²
- vᵧ = v₀ᵧ - gt

**Range & Height:**
- Range = v₀² sin(2θ)/g
- Max height = v₀² sin²(θ)/(2g)

#### Exam Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: Energy Conservation**
- "A mass is dropped from height h. Find velocity at ground level"
- Answer: v = √(2gh)

**Pattern 2: Spring Problems**
- "A mass on a spring is displaced 0.1 m and released. Find maximum velocity"
- Answer: v_max = ω·A = √(k/m)·A

**Pattern 3: Oscillation Problems**
- "Find the period of oscillation for a mass-spring system"
- Answer: T = 2π√(m/k)

#### Practice Strategy
1. Master energy conservation (do 20 problems)
2. Solve spring problems (do 15 problems)
3. Analyze projectile motion (do 15 problems)
4. Understand oscillations
5. Apply work-energy theorem
