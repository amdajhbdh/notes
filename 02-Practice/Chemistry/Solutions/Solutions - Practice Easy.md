# Solutions - Practice Easy

## Problem 1: Molarity Calculation
How many moles of NaCl are in 2 L of 0.5 M solution?

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

M = n/V
n = M * V = 0.5 * 2 = 1 mol
</details>

---

## Problem 2: Mass from Molarity
What mass of NaCl (M = 58.5 g/mol) is needed for 500 mL of 2 M solution?

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

n = M * V = 2 * 0.5 = 1 mol
mass = n * M = 1 * 58.5 = 58.5 g
</details>

---

## Problem 3: Dilution
Dilute 50 mL of 6 M HCl to 300 mL. What is the new molarity?

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

M_1V_1 = M_2V_2
6 * 50 = M_2 * 300
M_2 = 300/300 = 1 M
</details>

---

## Problem 4: Mass Percent
10 g salt dissolved in 90 g water. What is the mass percent?

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

% = (mass_solute/mass_solution) * 100%
= (10/100) * 100%
= 10%
</details>

---

## Problem 5: Volume for Dilution
How much water to add to 100 mL of 4 M solution to make it 1 M?

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

M_1V_1 = M_2V_2
4 * 100 = 1 * V_2
V_2 = 400 mL

Water to add = 400 - 100 = 300 mL
</details>

---

## Problem 6: Concentration Conversion
A solution has 20 g NaOH (M = 40 g/mol) in 500 mL. Find molarity.

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

n = mass/M = 20/40 = 0.5 mol
M = n/V = 0.5/0.5 = 1 M
</details>

---

## Problem 7: Mixing Solutions
Mix 100 mL of 2 M NaCl with 200 mL of 1 M NaCl. Find final molarity.

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

Total moles = (2 * 0.1) + (1 * 0.2) = 0.2 + 0.2 = 0.4 mol
Total volume = 100 + 200 = 300 mL = 0.3 L
M = 0.4/0.3 = 1.33 M
</details>

---

## Problem 8: Parts Per Million
5 mg pollutant in 1 L water (density = 1 g/mL). Find ppm.

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

1 L water = 1000 g
ppm = (5 mg / 1000 g) * 10**6
= (0.005 g / 1000 g) * 10**6
= 5 ppm
</details>
