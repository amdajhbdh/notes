# Dynamics - Springs Practice Easy

## Problems

### Problem 1: Hooke's Law

A spring with spring constant $k = 50$ N/m is stretched by 0.2 m. Find the restoring force.

**Solution:**
$$F = -kx = -50 \times 0.2 = -10 \text{ N}$$

---

### Problem 2: Finding Spring Constant

A 0.3 kg mass stretches a spring by 15 cm. Find the spring constant.

**Solution:**
At equilibrium: $mg = kx_0$
$$k = \frac{mg}{x_0} = \frac{0.3 \times 9.8}{0.15} = 19.6 \text{ N/m}$$

---

### Problem 3: Period of Oscillation

A 1 kg mass attached to a spring oscillates with period 2 s. Find the spring constant.

**Solution:**
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}} \Rightarrow k = \frac{4\pi^2 m}{T^2}$$
$$k = \frac{4\pi^2 \times 1}{2^2} = \frac{4\pi^2}{4} = \pi^2 \approx 9.87 \text{ N/m}$$

---

### Problem 4: Potential Energy

A spring with $k = 100$ N/m is compressed by 0.1 m. Find the potential energy stored.

**Solution:**
$$E_p = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 100 \times (0.1)^2 = 0.5 \text{ J}$$

---

### Problem 5: Maximum Velocity

A 0.5 kg mass on a spring ($k = 200$ N/m) is displaced 5 cm and released. Find maximum velocity.

**Solution:**
$$v_{max} = A\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} = 0.05 \times \sqrt{\frac{200}{0.5}} = 0.05 \times 20 = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

## Quick Review

| Formula | Description |
|---------|-------------|

| $k = mg/x$ | Spring constant from stretch |
| $T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k}$ | Period |

| $v_{max} = A\sqrt{k/m}$ | Maximum velocity |
