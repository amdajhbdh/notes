# Induction - Practice Easy

## Magnetic Flux Problems

### Problem 1

A rectangular coil with dimensions 0.1 m × 0.2 m is placed in a uniform magnetic field of 0.5 T. Calculate the magnetic flux when the coil is:

a) Perpendicular to the field
b) Parallel to the field

**Solution:**

a) When perpendicular: $\theta = 0°$, $\cos(0°) = 1$
$$\Phi = BA\cos\theta = 0.5 \times (0.1 \times 0.2) \times 1 = 0.01 \text{ Wb}$$

b) When parallel: $\theta = 90°$, $\cos(90°) = 0$
$$\Phi = 0.01 \times 0 = 0 \text{ Wb}$$

---

### Problem 2

A circular coil with radius 5 cm has 100 turns in a magnetic field of 0.2 T. Find the maximum magnetic flux through the coil.

**Solution:**
$$A = \pi r^2 = \pi \times (0.05)^2 = 7.85 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^2$$
$$\Phi_{max} = NBA = 100 \times 0.2 \times 7.85 \times 10^{-3} = 0.157 \text{ Wb}$$

## Faraday's Law Problems

### Problem 3

A straight conductor of length 0.3 m moves with a speed of 5 m/s perpendicular to a magnetic field. If the induced emf is 0.45 V, find the magnetic field strength.

**Solution:**
$$\mathcal{E} = Blv \Rightarrow B = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{lv}$$
$$B = \frac{0.45}{0.3 \times 5} = 0.3 \text{ T}$$

---

### Problem 4

A coil of 50 turns has a magnetic flux of 0.02 Wb passing through it. If the flux decreases to zero in 0.1 s, find the average induced emf.

**Solution:**
$$\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{\Delta\Phi}{\Delta t} = -50 \times \frac{0 - 0.02}{0.1} = 10 \text{ V}$$

---

### Problem 5

A rectangular loop (0.1 m × 0.2 m) rotates at 60 Hz in a 0.3 T magnetic field. Find the maximum emf induced.

**Solution:**
$$A = 0.1 \times 0.2 = 0.02 \text{ m}^2$$
$$\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi \times 60 = 120\pi \text{ rad/s}$$
$$\mathcal{E}_{max} = BA\omega = 0.3 \times 0.02 \times 120\pi = 2.26 \text{ V}$$

## Lenz's Law Problems

### Problem 6

A bar magnet is moved toward a coil. Determine the direction of the induced current in the coil as seen from the magnet's perspective.

**Solution:**
According to Lenz's law, the induced current creates a magnetic field that opposes the change. The coil will repel the approaching north pole, so the face of the coil facing the magnet becomes a north pole. Using the right-hand rule, this means the induced current flows counterclockwise as viewed from the magnet.

---

### Problem 7

A loop is pulled away from a region of constant magnetic field. State the direction of the induced current.

**Solution:**
As the loop leaves the magnetic field, the flux decreases. The induced current will create a magnetic field that tries to maintain the original flux, so it flows to attract the loop back. The current direction is opposite to what it was when entering the field.
